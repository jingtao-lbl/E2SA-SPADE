# DOE ECRP FY26, data preparation for the 26-event Alaska extreme-event catalog

## Scientific context

The DOE Office of Science Early Career Research Program FY26 full proposal (PI Jing Tao, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory; topic, *Energy, Land, and Human Interdependencies in Coastal-Urban or Coastal-Rural Systems within Earth and Environmental Systems Modeling*) proposes a hierarchical AI/ML-bridged modeling framework that couples three DOE physics models (the Energy Exascale Earth System Model Land Model ELM at 1 km regional grid, the Advanced Terrestrial Simulator ATS at 10 to 100 m hyper-resolution coupled surface-subsurface thermal hydrology, and MOSART/RDycore with the GPU-accelerated river dynamical core at 10 to 30 m flood inundation) through a region-specific Fourier-neural-operator emulator, with an agentic orchestrator A2MC-Evolve driving end-to-end calibration and a graph-neural-network AURORA module mapping coupled hazard fields to non-grid energy infrastructure risk indicators.

The proposal assembled a catalog of **26 Alaska historical extreme events** (locked 2026-05-26) spanning the four-Region coastal gradient (A North Slope, B Western Alaska + Yukon-Kuskokwim Delta, C Southcentral + Gulf of Alaska, D Southeast Alaska). The catalog is partitioned into **7 calibration events** that tune framework parameters and **19 validation events** withheld from parameter tuning for prediction-skill assessment. Within the 19 validation events, **5 are designated benchmark validation events** (one per Region or Region B sub-domain) that the proposal commits in writing to reproducing as Year-3 and Year-5 milestones. The remaining 14 thicken the per-Region pool for ensemble-skill statistics. The catalog spans three hazard types: **Type 1** heat / thermal anomaly (5 events at Regions A and D), **Type 2** coastal storm-surge / compound flooding (10 events at Regions A, B, and C), and **Type 3** atmospheric river plus extreme precipitation plus landslide plus drought plus glacial-lake outburst flood (**11 events** at Regions C and D as of 2026-05-26 with the E34 Snow River 2019 GLOF addition).

SPADE is positioned to deliver the **observational and forcing data backbone** for these 26 events. The SPADE permafrost backbone (ground ice content, active layer thickness, mean annual ground temperature, thaw event labels) directly serves Regions A and B where permafrost is the dominant subsurface modulator of extreme-event impact, and the SPADE agentic data pipeline extends naturally to atmospheric forcing assembly (HRRR-AK 3 km + Cheng et al. 2025 RASM-WRF 4 km), and to multi-source observation harmonization (NGEE-Arctic data via ESS-DIVE, DOE ARM site observations, USGS NWIS streamflow including gauge 15243900 Snow River near Seward, NOAA NWLON tide gauges, satellite InSAR via ASF Vertex, SMAP soil moisture + freeze-thaw, MODIS LST + snow cover, ArcticDEM time-stamped strips). 

Downstream framework components (ATS 3D coupled runs, MOSART/RDycore watershed routing + inundation, AURORA GNN vulnerability inference) consume the SPADE-prepared data but are outside SPADE's scope.

## Data preparation work requested

This task covers four work types; the agent may choose to execute them in phases.

### Work type 1, atmospheric forcing assembly: continuous 2000-2025 (priority: high)

Assemble continuous ELM-ready atmospheric forcing fields spanning **2000-01-01 through 2025-12-31** across the SPADE area of interest (the 4-Region Alaska coastal gradient). Downstream models read the continuous forcing field and select the simulation windows they need at runtime, including any per-event pre-event spin-up or post-event decay periods. The 26-event catalog drives the per-event OBSERVATION assembly (Work type 2 below); forcing itself is delivered as continuous time series, not as per-event slices.

**Forcing-source coverage and blending strategy.**

The two primary sources are blended along the HRRR-AK operational-availability boundary:

- **2000-01-01 through 2018-07-11**: primary source **Cheng et al. 2025** RASM-WRF Alaska + Yukon River Basin reanalysis at 4 km, accessed via NCAR Research Data Archive at doi:10.5065/ZPSB-PS82 (Cheng et al. 2024 dataset). Atmosphere variables hourly to daily, land variables 3-hourly to monthly. Cheng 2025 covers 1990-2021; SPADE consumes the 2000-2018-07-11 slice.
- **2018-07-12 through 2025-12-31**: primary source **HRRR-AK 3 km** via NOAA AWS Open Data and the Blaylock HRRR Archive. Native grid, hourly, 8 ELM-relevant variables (see table below). The 2018-07-12 boundary corresponds to the operational HRRRv3-AK start date.
- **2018-07-12 through 2021 (overlap)**: Cheng 2025 also covers this period and may be retained alongside HRRR-AK for cross-validation, but HRRR-AK is the primary source from 2018-07-12 onward.

Adapters: HRRR-AK adapter is to be developed; Cheng et al. 2025 NCAR-RDA adapter is to be developed.

**ELM-ready netCDF output specification.** Every deliverable must conform to the ELM (E3SM Land Model) atmospheric-forcing convention so the DOE ECRP framework's ELM 1 km regional component can ingest the files with no manual reformatting.

| ELM variable | Description | Unit | Source mapping (HRRR-AK / Cheng 2025) |
|---|---|---|---|
| `TBOT` | Reference-height air temperature | K | HRRR `TMP` at 2 m / Cheng `T2` |
| `PBOT` | Surface pressure | Pa | HRRR `PRES` surface / Cheng `PSFC` |
| `QBOT` (or `RH`) | Specific humidity (or relative humidity); ELM accepts either, prefer `QBOT` | kg kg-1 (or %) | derive from HRRR `SPFH` 2 m / Cheng `Q2` |
| `WIND` | Wind speed magnitude at reference height (sqrt(U^2+V^2)) | m s-1 | derive from HRRR `UGRD`+`VGRD` 10 m / Cheng `U10`+`V10` |
| `FSDS` | Downward shortwave radiation at surface | W m-2 | HRRR `DSWRF` / Cheng `SWDOWN` (convert J m-2 to W m-2 by dividing by accumulation interval if applicable) |
| `FLDS` | Downward longwave radiation at surface | W m-2 | HRRR `DLWRF` / Cheng `GLW` (same accumulation conversion if applicable) |
| `PRECTmms` | Total precipitation rate | mm s-1 | HRRR `APCP` accumulation -> rate / Cheng `RAINC`+`RAINNC` -> rate |
| `ZBOT` | Reference height for `TBOT`, `WIND`, `QBOT` | m | scalar attribute: 2.0 m for temperature/humidity, 10.0 m for wind (DATM splits into separate streams if needed); set ZBOT = 2.0 m for the temperature/humidity stream and document the 10 m wind reference in metadata |

**File structure.**

- **Calendar**: `noleap` (365-day calendar, no leap years). Document explicitly in `time:calendar` netCDF attribute.
- **Time units**: `days since 2000-01-01 00:00:00` (UTC). Single fixed reference epoch for the continuous 2000-2025 deliverable so downstream models can concatenate or slice freely.
- **Dimensions for site-point output (DATM single-point ELM runs)**: `(time, lat=1, lon=1)`. Lat/lon stored as float64 in WGS84 degrees.
- **Dimensions for gridded regional output (ELM 1 km regional grid, the DOE ECRP framework's primary mode)**: `(time, lat, lon)` on the framework's 1 km Alaska target grid. Regrid HRRR-AK 3 km -> ELM 1 km and Cheng 2025 4 km -> ELM 1 km using bilinear interpolation for state variables (T, P, Q, W) and first-order conservative remapping for flux variables (radiation, precipitation) to preserve volumetric and energetic budgets. Use `xesmf` or equivalent (CDO `remapcon`) and record the remap weights file with the deliverable.
- **Time resolution**: hourly for HRRR-AK and Cheng 2025 atmosphere variables. Subdaily ELM runs use hourly forcing directly; daily-step ELM runs aggregate within the adapter (mean for state, sum for accumulated flux).
- **Continuous packaging**: per-year netCDF files per source (`forcing_<source>_<YYYY>.nc`), all 8 variables co-located. Annual files keep individual file sizes tractable (~250 GB/year for HRRR-AK at native AK grid; smaller for Cheng 2025) while supporting fast random-access slicing for downstream models. Files are concatenable along the time dimension. Example: `forcing_HRRRv4AK_2022.nc`, `forcing_Cheng2025_2010.nc`. The full deliverable set is 26 years × 2 sources = up to 52 files (fewer where one source does not cover a given year).
- **Global attributes**: `forcing_source` (HRRRv4AK / HRRRv3AK / Cheng2025), `time_coverage_start`, `time_coverage_end`, `source_doi_or_url`, `regrid_method`, `e2sa_adapter_version`, `e2sa_schema_version`, `creation_time_utc`. No per-event attributes (event_id, event_name, region, hazard_type) since the forcing field is continuous; models look up event windows from the 26-event catalog table at runtime.
- **Per-variable attributes**: `units`, `long_name`, `standard_name` (CF-compliant), `_FillValue` (use `NaN` for floats, never magic numbers).

The per-variable units and naming above match the E3SM DATM forcing convention used by ELM for single-point and gridded simulations. Adapter implementations should validate units explicitly at write time, not rely on convention.

### Work type 3, surface dataset assembly (priority: medium)

Assemble ELM-ready surface-dataset netCDF files for every event in the catalog. ELM's surface dataset (the `surfdata_*_simyr*_c*.nc` file in the E3SM input-data tree) carries the static-state inputs the land model needs at initialization, namely PFT composition, soil texture and organic content profiles, land-unit fractions (lake, glacier, urban, wetland, crop), and topography. For permafrost-relevant sites the surface dataset is also where ground-ice-aware initial conditions enter the framework via custom-built fields layered on top of the standard surfdata structure.

**Two-path assembly.** Surface datasets for each event should be assembled along one of two paths, chosen per site based on in-situ data availability. The agent surveys local inventory at Step 0 before deciding which path applies to each site.

**Path A: site-specific assembly from in-situ measurements.** Use this path where the event domain overlaps a site with characterized soil profiles and vegetation surveys. Override the ELM default fields at those grid cells with measured values.

- *Soil texture profile*: percent sand, silt, clay per soil layer from site core sampling. Map to ELM `PCT_SAND` and `PCT_CLAY` at each of the ELM soil-layer depths (default 15-layer ELM column from 0 to ~42 m, with active-layer-dense top 0-3 m).
- *Soil organic content profile*: organic carbon density per soil layer (kg C m-3). Map to ELM `ORGANIC`. NGEE-Arctic intensive sites (Kougarok, Council, Teller, Barrow / Utqiagvik, Atqasuk) have published soil characterization; cite the per-DOI ESS-DIVE record in the provenance.
- *PFT composition*: percent cover per PFT from in-situ vegetation survey. Map to ELM `PCT_PFT` across the framework's PFT list (default 16 PFTs in E3SM; the framework may have custom PFT extensions for Arctic vegetation, confirm before mapping). NGEE-Arctic plot-scale vegetation surveys at Seward Peninsula sites and ABoVE field-vegetation plots are the primary sources for the Region A and B sites.
- *Land-unit fractions*: where the site has documented lake / wetland / glacier fractions, override `PCT_LAKE`, `PCT_WETLAND`, `PCT_GLACIER`. Otherwise inherit from Path B.

**Path B: extract from ELM global surface dataset.** Use this path where no in-situ characterization exists for the site. Subset the standard ELM global surfdata file to the event domain.

- *Source file*: the E3SM-released global surface dataset matching the framework's ELM version pinning. Default candidate: `surfdata_360x720cru_simyr1850_<version>.nc` (or whatever resolution and base year the DOE ECRP framework commits to). For 1 km regional Alaska runs, the framework uses a remapped variant or the higher-resolution `surfdata_0.125x0.125_<version>.nc`; confirm the pinned file path in the framework's namelist before subsetting.
- *Location*: E3SM input-data tree (`inputdata/lnd/clm2/surfdata_map/` on NERSC/OLCF/ACES compute, or via the E3SM input-data distribution).
- *Subset method*: extract the lat/lon box covering the event domain, preserve all variables (PCT_PFT, PCT_LAKE, PCT_WETLAND, PCT_GLACIER, PCT_URBAN, PCT_CROP, ORGANIC, PCT_SAND, PCT_CLAY, TOPO, LANDFRAC, etc.) without modification. Use `xarray` for slicing; do not regrid (preserve native resolution to avoid double-interpolation artifacts when ELM regrids internally).

**Permafrost-specific initial-condition layer (Regions A and B, on top of either path).** For events in permafrost-dominant Regions (A, B Seward Pen. + Norton Sound + Y-K Delta), include additional permafrost initial-condition fields that are not part of the standard ELM surfdata. These layer on top of the assembled surfdata as a per-event auxiliary netCDF that the framework's permafrost spin-up component consumes.

- Ground ice content profile from the Brown et al. 2002 circum-Arctic ground ice map (`permafrost_ground_ice_map.md`), supplemented by the SPADE physics-constrained generative ice-content model output once available (SPADE primary deliverable, target September 2026).
- Permafrost extent classification (continuous, discontinuous, sporadic, isolated) from the Brown et al. 2002 map.
- Active layer thickness baseline from CALM at applicable sites (`calm.md`).
- Mean annual ground temperature baseline from GTN-P at applicable sites (`gtnp.md`) and UAF GIPL (`gipl_uaf_permafrost.md`).
- Thaw event labels at the event window from the Alaska Permafrost Thaw Database (Webb et al. 2026, 19,540 labeled points; `alaska_thaw_db.md`).
- Thermokarst features at the event window from the Olefeldt 2016 circumpolar map (`thermokarst_circumpolar.md`).

**Per-Region application of the two paths.**

- **Region A (North Slope, 5 events)**: Path A primary at NGEE-Arctic Barrow / Utqiagvik / Atqasuk / Drew Point and at ABoVE / ARM Oliktok footprints; Path B fallback for the remainder of the 1 km Region A domain. Permafrost initial-condition layer applies throughout.
- **Region B (Western Alaska + Y-K Delta, 9 events)**: Path A primary at NGEE-Arctic Kougarok / Council / Teller on the Seward Peninsula; Path B for the rest of Region B (no NGEE-Arctic intensive sites in the Y-K Delta sub-domain). Permafrost initial-condition layer applies throughout.
- **Region C (Southcentral + Gulf of Alaska, 3 events)**: Path B throughout. No NGEE-Arctic or ARM at this Region. Supplement with GIPL borehole records at Gakona / Gulkana for the permafrost initial-condition layer where the Middle Copper HUC-8 overlaps mapped permafrost extent (limited, mostly discontinuous to isolated).
- **Region D (Southeast Alaska, 9 events)**: Path B throughout. Mostly non-permafrost. Skip the permafrost initial-condition layer for this Region. PFT overrides from local vegetation maps where they exist (Tongass forest inventory, Sitka and Juneau community vegetation surveys).

**Output format.** Per-event surfdata netCDF following the ELM surfdata convention (same dimensions, variable names, and units as the framework's input file). Filename convention: `surfdata_<event_id>_<region>_<path>_<creation_date>.nc`. Example: `surfdata_E07_B-YKDelta_pathB_20260521.nc`. For Path A sites, the netCDF embeds the in-situ override provenance (which NGEE-Arctic DOI, which CALM site code, which ABoVE plot id) in per-grid-cell metadata so downstream reviewers can audit the override chain. Permafrost initial-condition layer ships as a companion file `permafrost_ic_<event_id>_<region>_<creation_date>.nc` referenced by the surfdata file via a global attribute.

## Acceptance criteria

This task is `done` when:

1. **MANIFEST.md** lists every produced deliverable with absolute path and sha256: the continuous 2000-2025 forcing files (per-year per-source), plus per-event observation bundles, per-event surface-dataset priors, and per-event QC reports for each of the 26 events.

3. Variable units and conventions match the ELM-specific defaults documented in `projects/spade/tasks/CLAUDE.md` (single-point DATM-style netCDF, calendar noleap unless otherwise specified, time units `days since YYYY-MM-DD 00:00:00`, WGS84 lat/lon).
4. The two SPADE adapters for HRRR-AK and for the Cheng et al. 2025 NCAR-RDA dataset are added under `e2sa/data/` with documentation in `projects/spade/data/sources/`.
5. The deliverable set is suitable for direct ingestion by the ELM (and downstream ATS + MOSART/RDycore) component of the DOE ECRP framework when funded; no manual reformatting required.
6. The companion `MANIFEST.md` provides a complete provenance record for downstream consumers.

## Notes and constraints

- **License.** The catalog itself (the table, the role assignments, the type tags) is original work by Jing Tao under the DOE ECRP FY26 effort. References cited in the table point to their respective publishers and licensing terms. Upstream observation and forcing datasets (HRRR-AK, Cheng et al. 2025, ERA5, NGEE-Arctic, ARM, CALM, GTN-P, USGS NWIS, NOAA NWLON, Sentinel-1, ABoVE, etc.) are accessed under their respective public-domain or open-data licenses; per-source license terms are propagated through SPADE's provenance schema.
- **Catalog stability invariants.** Event IDs E04, E19, E20, E21, E22, E23, E25, E29 are intentionally unused; never reuse. The 5 benchmark validation events (E01, E08, E07, **E34 (was E32 until 2026-05-26)**, E11) are committed in writing in the DOE ECRP FY26 proposal as Year-3 and Year-5 milestone events; never demote without explicit Jing sign-off and an amendment block. The 7 + 19 calibration / validation split anchors the framework's out-of-sample skill protocol; never blend the two cohorts without explicit Jing sign-off.
- **Out-of-scope hazard types.** Type 4 (river-ice / spring break-up / ice-jam / aufeis) was dropped from the catalog because the ELM-ATS-MOSART/RDycore stack does not have channelized river-ice physics. Wildfire-driven hazards were dropped because the framework's Type 3 archetype is defined around AR-driven precipitation extremes, landslides, drought, and GLOF rather than fire-weather. 
- **Forcing-source acquisition footprint.** The continuous 2000-2025 deliverable is materially larger than the previous per-event approach. HRRR-AK at native 3 km Alaska grid spans 2018-07-12 through 2025 and is estimated at **~1.5-2 TB** for the SPADE area of interest (the 4-Region Alaska coastal gradient), packaged as ~250 GB per annual file × ~7-8 years. Cheng et al. 2025 at 4 km Alaska + Yukon River Basin spans 1990-2021; the SPADE 2000-2018-07-11 slice is estimated at **~5-15 TB** for the AOI depending on the spatial extraction strategy (full Alaska-coastal subset vs HUC-8 sub-watershed subset). Total continuous-2000-2025 forcing footprint: **~7-17 TB**, much larger than the prior ~880 GB per-event estimate. The SPADE implementation team should confirm the spatial-extraction strategy and size NERSC project storage accordingly before bulk download. NCAR-RDA and NOAA AWS Open Data downloads are typically no-cost; the agent should surface any unforeseen API charges before proceeding.
- **Adapter development priority.** The HRRR-AK adapter and the Cheng et al. 2025 NCAR-RDA adapter do not yet exist in `e2sa/data/`. Building these two adapters is a co-deliverable of this task and a reusable contribution to SPADE beyond the DOE ECRP event catalog.
- **Related upstream task.** SPADE's primary deliverable (Alaska ground-ice content map ensemble from the physics-constrained generative model) provides the surface-dataset initial-condition layer for Regions A and B in Work type 3 above. This task is an "anchor consumer" of SPADE's primary deliverable; the 26-event catalog is the validation footprint that the SPADE ice content map ensemble will be evaluated against.
